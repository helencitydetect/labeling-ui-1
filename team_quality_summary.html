Section 8

Reviewer Comment

The reviewed sample for this period shows a clear and consistent team-wide pattern: under-labeling is the primary quality issue, accounting for 73% of all corrections. The team generally performs adequately on structural conditions that are explicitly visible, but consistently misses secondary conditions — particularly lawn zone attributes and less prominent structural issues — on images that already contain other labeled conditions.

A second recurring issue is the confusion between bad-picture attributes, affecting roughly a third of the team. This is a straightforward calibration problem that can likely be resolved with a short session and clear visual examples.

The two annotators with the lowest exact match rates (leonardm at 28% and maxwellt at 31%) share a dominant pattern of bad-picture label confusion and warrant individual attention before the next period. The rest of the team clusters around the 40–60% range, which is improvable through the team-level actions recommended above.
